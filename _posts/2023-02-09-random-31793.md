<p>Norovirus is a highly contagious virus that is responsible for causing gastrointestinal illness, commonly referred to as the “stomach flu”. It is the most common cause of acute gastroenteritis in the United States, causing an estimated 19-21 million cases of illness each year. Norovirus is spread through contact with an infected person, contaminated food or water, or contaminated surfaces. Symptoms of norovirus infection include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach cramping, and sometimes fever, headaches, and body aches.</p>
<p>Norovirus is a member of the Caliciviridae family of viruses. It is a small non-enveloped virus that is resistant to many common disinfectants and can survive temperatures as low as -20°C. It is highly contagious and can be spread through direct contact with an infected person, consuming contaminated food or water, or touching contaminated surfaces. The virus is shed in the stool of infected people and can remain infectious on surfaces for several days.</p>
<p>Norovirus is most commonly spread through contact with an infected person. People can become infected with norovirus if they have direct contact with the vomit or stool of an infected person. This can occur through close contact such as sharing food or drinks, or through contact with contaminated surfaces. People can also become infected with norovirus if they eat contaminated food or drink contaminated water. Contaminated food can include fruits and vegetables that have been contaminated with norovirus, or food that has been prepared by an infected person who did not wash their hands properly.</p>
<p>The symptoms of norovirus infection usually begin within one to two days after exposure and can last for up to three days. Symptoms include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach cramping, and sometimes fever, headaches, and body aches. Most people recover without treatment, but some may require medical attention if their symptoms become severe.</p>
<p>The best way to prevent norovirus infection is to practice good hand hygiene. This includes washing hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds before and after preparing food, after using the bathroom, and after coming into contact with an infected person. It is also important to avoid consuming contaminated food or water and to clean and disinfect contaminated surfaces.</p>
