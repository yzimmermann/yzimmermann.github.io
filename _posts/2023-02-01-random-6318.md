<p>Hulk Hogan, born Terry Gene Bollea, is a professional wrestler and actor who is widely regarded as one of the most influential figures in the history of professional wrestling. He is best known for his time in the World Wrestling Federation (WWF) and World Championship Wrestling (WCW) between 1984 and 2000.</p>
<p>Hogan first gained fame in the 1980s as the face of the WWF's "Rock 'n' Wrestling" connection, a combination of wrestling and pop culture that was heavily marketed to children and teenagers. He was the most recognized wrestler in the world, and his entrance music and catchphrase, "Whatcha gonna do, brother?", became iconic. He headlined the first WrestleMania event in 1985, and his matches against Andre the Giant, The Iron Sheik, and Macho Man Randy Savage are considered classics.</p>
<p>Hogan left the WWF in 1993 and joined WCW, where he was a major force in the company's rise to become the top wrestling promotion in the United States. He held the WCW World Heavyweight Championship a record six times, and was the leader of the influential New World Order (nWo) stable. He returned to the WWF in 2002, and held the WWE Championship three times before retiring in 2003.</p>
<p>Hogan has also had a successful acting career, appearing in films such as Rocky III and No Holds Barred, and starring in his own television show, Thunder in Paradise. He was inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame in 2005.</p>
<p>Hogan is also known for his philanthropic work, having founded the Hogan's Beach Shop charity in 2007, which has raised millions of dollars for various causes. He is also a frequent guest speaker at various wrestling conventions and events, and is a regular on the Howard Stern show.</p>
<p>Hogan has become an iconic figure in the wrestling world, and his influence on the industry has been immense. His style and charisma have inspired countless wrestlers over the years, and his matches and feuds are still discussed today. He is a true icon of professional wrestling, and his legacy will live on for years to come.</p>
