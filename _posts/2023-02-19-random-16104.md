<p>Jimmy Carter is a former United States President, Nobel Peace Prize winner, and humanitarian. He served as the 39th President of the United States from 1977 to 1981 and is widely respected for his commitment to human rights and international peace. He was born on October 1, 1924 in Plains, Georgia and was the first president to be born in a hospital.</p>
<p>Carter attended the United States Naval Academy and served in the Navy from 1946 to 1953. After leaving the Navy, he returned to Plains and ran his family’s peanut farm. He later served two terms in the Georgia State Senate and was elected Governor of Georgia in 1971.</p>
<p>In 1976, Carter ran for president and won the election in a landslide victory. As president, Carter pushed for the Panama Canal Treaties and the Camp David Accords, which led to a peace agreement between Israel and Egypt. He also worked to improve relations with the Soviet Union and signed the SALT II Treaty, which limited the number of nuclear weapons each country could possess.</p>
<p>Carter also focused on domestic issues, including energy conservation, civil service reform, and deregulation of the airline industry. He also established the Department of Education and signed the Alaska National Interest Lands Conservation Act, which preserved more than 100 million acres of land.</p>
<p>Despite his accomplishments, Carter’s presidency was marred by economic problems, the Iran hostage crisis, and the Soviet invasion of Afghanistan. He lost the 1980 presidential election to Ronald Reagan.</p>
<p>After leaving office, Carter founded the Carter Center, a non-profit organization dedicated to promoting human rights and democracy around the world. He also wrote several books and has been involved in numerous humanitarian efforts, including monitoring elections in various countries and working to eradicate diseases such as Guinea worm.</p>
<p>In 2002, Carter was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his work in promoting peace, democracy, and human rights. He is widely respected for his commitment to these causes and his tireless efforts to make the world a better place.</p>
