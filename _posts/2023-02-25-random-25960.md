<p>The Los Angeles Clippers are an American professional basketball team based in Los Angeles, California. The Clippers compete in the National Basketball Association (NBA) as a member of the Pacific Division of the league's Western Conference. The Clippers play their home games at the Staples Center, an arena shared with fellow NBA team the Los Angeles Lakers, as well as the Los Angeles Sparks of the Women's National Basketball Association (WNBA).</p>
<p>The franchise was founded in 1970 as the Buffalo Braves, one of three expansion teams to join the NBA that year. The Braves moved from Buffalo, New York to San Diego, California in 1978 and became known as the San Diego Clippers. In 1984, the Clippers moved to Los Angeles. Through much of its history, the franchise failed to see significant regular season or playoff success. The Clippers were frequently seen as an example of a perennial loser in American professional sports, drawing unfavorable comparisons to their in-city rival Lakers, who have won 16 NBA championships.</p>
<p>The Clippers' fortunes turned in the early 2010s with the acquisition of core players Blake Griffin, DeAndre Jordan, and Chris Paul. In 2013, the franchise won its first division title, as the team made the playoffs for the ninth time in franchise history and the third time in the previous eight seasons. They also added to their budding rivalry with the Lakers, as they finished with a better record than the Lakers for the fifth time and won the season series for the second time since moving to Los Angeles in 1984, this time in a sweep. They repeated as division champions in 2014.</p>
<p>The Clippers continued to be regular playoff contenders in the following years, but they were unable to make the Conference Finals during the Paul–Griffin–Jordan era. They also failed to make the playoffs in the following two seasons. In the summer of 2017, the franchise changed its core when they traded away Chris Paul and released/traded away other key players, such as Jamal Crawford, J. J. Redick, and Luc Mbah a Moute. The franchise also hired a new head coach, Doc Rivers, and acquired players such as Patrick Beverley, Lou Williams, Montrezl Harrell, and Danilo Gallinari during the 2017 offseason.</p>
<p>The Clippers went on to make the playoffs in the 2017–18 season, but were eliminated in the first round by the Utah Jazz. The following season, the Clippers signed Kawhi Leonard and traded for Paul George, forming one of the most star-studded duos in the league. The team went on to make the playoffs in the 2019–20 season and advanced to the Conference Finals for the first time in franchise history. Despite the success, the Clippers were eliminated by the Denver Nuggets in the Conference Semifinals of the 2020 playoffs.</p>
<p>The Los Angeles Clippers have experienced a significant turnaround in recent years, going from perennial losers to legitimate contenders in the NBA. The team has been able to acquire key players through trades and free agency, and have been able to develop a strong culture and identity. With the addition of Kawhi Leonard and Paul George, the Clippers have become one of the most talented teams in the NBA, and are poised to make a deep run in the playoffs in the coming years.</p>
