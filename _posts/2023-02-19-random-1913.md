<p>Jimmy Carter is a former president of the United States, and a Nobel Peace Prize laureate. He is widely respected for his commitment to human rights and social justice. Born in Plains, Georgia, Carter attended the United States Naval Academy and served in the United States Navy before entering politics. He was elected governor of Georgia in 1970 and was elected president in 1976.</p>
<p>During his presidency, Carter worked to address the challenges of the Cold War and the energy crisis. He also worked to improve civil rights and human rights, and to promote peace in the Middle East. He established diplomatic relations with China and signed the Panama Canal Treaty. He also created the Department of Energy and the Department of Education.</p>
<p>Following his presidency, Carter continued to be active in public life. He founded The Carter Center, a non-profit organization that works to promote human rights and democracy. He has also been involved in numerous humanitarian efforts, including the eradication of Guinea worm disease, the promotion of democracy in Haiti, and the monitoring of elections in numerous countries. In 2002, he was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his efforts.</p>
<p>Carter has also written numerous books, including his autobiography, An Hour Before Daylight, and his memoirs, White House Diary. He has also written books on international relations and human rights, including Palestine: Peace Not Apartheid, We Can Have Peace in the Holy Land, and Beyond the White House.</p>
<p>Carter is a strong advocate of nuclear disarmament and has been critical of the United States’ use of unmanned drones. He has also been critical of the current administration’s policies on immigration and climate change. He has been vocal in his support of the Paris Agreement and the Iran nuclear deal.</p>
<p>Jimmy Carter is a living example of what it means to be an active citizen and a leader in the international community. He has dedicated his life to promoting human rights, social justice, and peace. He is an inspiration to many, and his legacy will continue to influence future generations.</p>
