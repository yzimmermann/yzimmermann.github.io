<p>Elvis Presley is one of the most iconic figures in music history. He was a singer, songwriter, actor, and cultural icon who rose to fame in the 1950s and 60s. His music and style helped define an era and he remains one of the most influential musicians of all time.</p>
<p>Presley was born in Tupelo, Mississippi on January 8, 1935. His parents, Vernon and Gladys, were poor and Elvis was raised in a humble home. As a child, Presley was exposed to the gospel music of the local churches and the blues of the nearby Mississippi Delta. He was also influenced by the country music of his parents' generation, and the popular music of the day.</p>
<p>Presley's career began in 1954 when he was signed to Sun Records by producer Sam Phillips. His first single, "That's All Right," was an instant hit and helped launch the rock and roll movement. Presley quickly gained a following and released a string of successful singles, including "Hound Dog," "Jailhouse Rock," and "Heartbreak Hotel."</p>
<p>In the late 1950s, Presley began to appear in movies, including Jailhouse Rock and King Creole. His films were popular and helped make him a household name.</p>
<p>In the 1960s, Presley continued to record and perform, but his music had become more mainstream. He released a series of popular albums, such as Elvis Is Back! and From Elvis in Memphis. He also starred in a number of films, including Viva Las Vegas and Blue Hawaii.</p>
<p>Presley was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1986 and is widely considered one of the most influential musicians of all time. His music has been covered by countless artists, from The Beatles to Bruce Springsteen. He is also credited with helping to bridge the gap between white and black music in the 1950s and 60s.</p>
<p>Presley died on August 16, 1977 at the age of 42. His death shocked the world, but his legacy lives on. His music, style, and influence continue to be felt in popular culture today. He remains one of the most iconic figures in music history.</p>
