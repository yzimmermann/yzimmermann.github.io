<p>Dolly Parton is an American singer, songwriter, multi-instrumentalist, record producer, actress, author, businesswoman, and philanthropist. She is best known for her iconic country music career, which has spanned more than five decades.</p>
<p>Parton was born in Sevierville, Tennessee, in 1946. She was the fourth of twelve children born to Robert Lee Parton and Avie Lee Parton. Parton began performing as a young child, singing on local radio and television programs in the East Tennessee area. She was inspired by the music of her father, who was a Pentecostal preacher and a musician.</p>
<p>Parton moved to Nashville in 1964 to pursue her musical career. She quickly gained recognition in the country music scene, and in 1967, she released her first single, "Dumb Blonde." The song became a Top Ten hit and established Parton as a star in the country music world.</p>
<p>In the 1970s, Parton began to expand her musical repertoire, releasing a string of pop and gospel albums. She also began to write and produce her own music, and in 1974, she released her first solo album, "Jolene." The title track became her biggest hit and is still one of her signature songs.</p>
<p>Parton continued to release successful albums throughout the 1980s and 1990s. In the late 1980s and early 1990s, she also began to focus on acting, appearing in films such as "Steel Magnolias" (1989) and "Straight Talk" (1992).</p>
<p>Parton has won numerous awards throughout her career, including nine Grammy Awards, two Academy of Country Music Awards, and two Country Music Association Awards. She has also been inducted into the Country Music Hall of Fame and the Songwriters Hall of Fame.</p>
<p>Parton is also known for her philanthropy. She founded the Dollywood Foundation in 1988, which provides educational opportunities for children in her home state of Tennessee. She has also been involved in numerous charitable causes, such as the American Red Cross and the Children's Miracle Network.</p>
<p>Parton is an icon of country music, and her influence is still felt today. Her music has transcended generations, and her unique sound and style continue to inspire new artists. Parton is a living legend, and her legacy will continue to live on for many years to come.</p>
